Flash Skills Workshop

Curriculum

These sessions build upon the GovLab’s experience training and coaching thousands of “public entrepreneurs” in those equitable, participatory and collaborative skills needed to implement social change in practice. Our commitment to training and coaching is rooted in the belief that when we work differently -- using better data, drawing on a wider range of experience and expertise and learning from what works elsewhere -- we are able to solve problems in practice more effectively. But such a transformation to new ways of working will not happen by itself. Social innovators must (and can!) learn how to work differently.

In each class, participants will receive practical instruction with clear, written learning goals, explanation of the value proposition for each skill, tips and tricks for applying the skill to one’s own project and at least one interactive worksheet that participants can use on their own.

1. Problem Definition
 In the face of increasingly complex challenges, community leaders must innovate in how we solve public problems. Before rushing to identify solutions, it is imperative to adequately define the problem. This session will focus on learning how to get past a vague statement of an issue to articulate an actionable problem that lends itself to concrete, implementable, and successful solutions.

2. Defining the Problem through Fast Field Scanning
 Fast field scanning - or rapid evidence review - refers to the skill of quickly getting up to speed on existing solutions to a problem. You will learn how to find solutions that have been tried before, how to assess the success of those solutions, and better evaluate whether solutions that worked elsewhere may help you in your work.

3. Designing Your Theory of Change
 Theory of Change is the skill of mapping how a solution is designed to respond to and address the problem. Theory of change asks you to work backwards from the solution to identify the necessary steps for change to happen and to lay out the underlying assumptions you’ve made. In the process, participants develop a more detailed understanding of the workflow needed to implement change and any potential risks that might arise as a result of incorrect assumptions.

4. Solving Public Problems with Open Innovation
 Open innovation describes the distributed process of working across organizational boundaries to accelerate innovation. Open innovation enhances both the effectiveness and legitimacy of problem solving. You will learn more about collective intelligence methods and understand how you may incorporate collective intelligence into an upcoming project.

5. Creating the Killer Memo
 To implement your projects, you will need to persuade people at every step of the process. Memos can help pitch your projects to participants, funders, and managers. You will learn strategies for persuasive memo writing to help you advance your work, a current project or a new idea.
